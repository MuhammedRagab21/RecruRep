# Feature Specification: RecruRep Landing Page

## Overview
A single-page marketing website for RecruRep — a SaaS platform that lets non-native English teachers apply to teaching jobs with one click, eliminating the need to switch between multiple apps, websites, and Facebook groups.

## Target Audience
Non-native English teachers (ages 22–45) currently piecing together job applications across:
- Job boards (Dave's ESL Cafe, TEFL.com)
- Recruitment agency websites
- Facebook Groups (ESL job groups)
- School/cram school websites

## Sections

### 1. Navigation
- Sticky top bar with logo ("RecruRep"), desktop nav links (Features, How It Works, Pricing, FAQ), and an emerald CTA button ("Get Early Access").
- Mobile: hamburger menu that slides in from right.

### 2. Hero Section
- Eyebrow tag: "FOR NON-NATIVE ENGLISH TEACHERS"
- Headline (display-xl, 60px): "Apply to teaching jobs in one click. No more app hopping."
- Subheadline (body-lg): "RecruRep brings every ESL teaching job into one platform. See a job you like? Press apply. Your profile, your documents, your cover letter — all sent in seconds."
- Primary CTA: "Join the Waitlist" (emerald button)
- Secondary CTA: "See Available Jobs" (outline button)
- Visual: A clean mockup showing a teacher's dashboard with job cards and a one-click apply button.

### 3. Problem / Pain Points (optional section before features)
- "The application shuffle" — 3 brief pain-point cards showing the current broken workflow
- Card 1: "Jumping between 5+ job sites daily"
- Card 2: "Re-entering the same info over and over"
- Card 3: "Missing out while info is scattered in Facebook groups"

### 4. Features / How It Works (3-step or 4-card grid)
- Card 1: "One-Click Apply" — Upload your profile once, apply to any job instantly
- Card 2: "All Jobs, One Place" — Curated ESL teaching jobs from schools worldwide, no more tab-hoarding
- Card 3: "Direct to Employer" — Applications go straight to the hiring school, no middleman
- Card 4: "Track Everything" — See your application status, interview invites, and offer letters in one dashboard

### 5. Social Proof / Testimonials
- 3 testimonial cards with avatar, name, location, and quote
- Format: Avatar circle (placeholder) + Name + Flag emoji + Quote
- Quotes should reflect real pain points: saving time, finding better jobs, reduced stress

### 6. Pricing
- 3 tiers in a card grid: Free, Basic, Pro
- Free: Browse jobs, 5 applications/month
- Basic ($9/mo): Unlimited applications, cover letter AI assist, priority support
- Pro ($19/mo): Everything in Basic + AI-powered profile optimization, application insights, featured applicant status
- Featured tier highlighted with emerald border

### 7. FAQ
- Accordion-style (click to expand)
- 6-8 questions covering:
  - Who can use RecruRep?
  - How does one-click apply work?
  - Do I need a work visa?
  - Is RecruRep free?
  - What countries have jobs?
  - Can I use it on my phone?
  - How is this different from job boards?
  - Do I need teaching experience?

### 8. Waitlist / Contact Section
- Email signup form with name + email + preferred teaching location (select)
- "Join 500+ teachers already on the waitlist" social proof line
- After submit: thank-you message with preview of what's coming

### 9. Footer
- Logo, short tagline
- Quick links
- Social links (placeholder)
- Copyright

## Design Reference
See `DESIGN.md` (VoltAgent-inspired dark theme).
- Canvas: #101010
- Emerald: #00d992
- Ink: #f2f2f2
- Inter font family
- Hairline borders (#3d3a39), no shadows
- 6px button radius, 8px card radius

## Copy Guidelines
- Warm, empathetic, teacher-first tone
- Short sentences. Active voice.
- No jargon. "Apply" not "submit your candidacy."
- Emphasize time saved, reduced friction, opportunity access
